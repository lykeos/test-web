Stroke Prediction

Project Information

In this project, I developed a Machine Learning model to predict strokes. The aim was to assist in identifying potential stroke risks based on various health parameters.

Project Objectives

The primary goal was to create a predictive model that could analyze health data and provide early indications of stroke risk factors.

My Role

I was responsible as one of the team members in the data science team (my project team).Tasks I handled included:

  1. Collecting and processing health data (Dataset from Kaggle).
  2. Conducting Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to identify key factors.
  3. Performing data preprocessing by handling missing values and outliers.
  4. Implementing Feature Engineering by encoding and scaling.
  5. Balancing the dataset using SMOTEENN.
  6. Conducting hyperparameter tuning to enhance model performance.
  7. Testing for Overfitting in the model.

Conclusion

Outcome

The model successfully predicted potential stroke cases with a Recall score of 82% after addressing overfitting through hyperparameter tuning. I managed to improve the model's performance by 42% and reduce the potential overfitting to 13% from 60%.

Conclusion

The developed model provides a valuable tool for healthcare professionals to identify potential stroke cases and intervene early, reducing the risk of severe consequences.

Insights

  1. Correlation between Stroke and Age, Hypertension, Heart Disease, and Glucose Level has a strong relationship in this dataset.
  2. The correlation between age and BMI is around 0.33. This indicates a moderate positive correlation between age and BMI. In other words, as age increases, BMI tends to increase in a stronger correlation than with blood glucose.
  3. There is a weak positive correlation between age and average blood glucose. It means that with increasing age, average blood glucose tends to slightly increase, although the correlation is weak.
  4. The correlation between average blood glucose and BMI is about 0.18. This indicates a weak positive correlation between average blood glucose and BMI. It means individuals with higher BMI tend to have slightly higher average blood glucose, although the correlation is weak.

Recommendations

  1. Implement the model in healthcare systems for routine screenings to detect potential stroke cases.
  2. Educate individuals with identified risk factors about lifestyle changes to mitigate stroke risks.
