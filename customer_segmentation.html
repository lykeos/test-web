Customer Segmentation

Project Information

In this project, I developed a Machine Learning model to cluster customers into several segments. The aim is to assist stakeholders in making decisions regarding suitable promotional strategies for each customer cluster to minimize profit declines within the company.

Project Objectives

The main objective is to create a clustering model capable of grouping customers into several segments by analyzing their recent transaction history, frequency, and transaction values for each customer.

My Role

I was responsible as a data scientist for:

  1. Cleaning data from missing values and illogical data.
  2. Performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to discover insights and patterns.
  3. Conducting feature engineering by creating new tables, encoding features, and scaling.
  4. Creating a clustering model using the KMeans algorithm.

Conclusion

Outcome

This model successfully clustered customers into 6 clusters based on their recent transaction history, frequency, and the total value of transactions conducted by each customer.

Conclusion

The developed model can assist stakeholders in devising appropriate promotional strategies for their customers, aiming to reduce profit decline resulting from ineffective promotional strategies.

Insights

  1. Cluster 3 and 4 represent clusters with high RFM scores, although the total number of customers in these two clusters is only 10, consisting of both non-UK and UK customers.
  2. Cluster 2 comprises customers exclusively outside the UK, accounting for the highest number of customers. However, customers from clusters 3 (CustomerID: 12415, 14156, 14646) and 4 (CustomerID: 14911) exhibit significantly higher purchase values. It might be beneficial to employ different promotional strategies, such as offering exclusive services.
  3. Cluster 1 has the lowest RFM score despite being the second-largest cluster, accounting for 22.07% of total customers, consisting of exclusively UK-based homo-customers.
  4. Cluster 0 ranks second-lowest in RFM scores despite being the largest cluster, representing 53.93% of total customers, comprising only UK-based homo-customers.
  5. Clusters 3 and 4 consist of customers who make frequent and substantial purchases. The majority of profits are generated from Clusters 3 and 4.
  6. There are few customers who spend their money shopping at this company.
  7. Approximately only 10 active customers shop here every week (Clusters 3 and 4).
  8. Non-UK customers also significantly impact our company's revenue.
  9. Our product can compete and attract interest from customers outside the UK.
  10. Behavioral hypothesis: This might be influenced by purchase taxes and shipping costs, leading non-UK customers to make frequent purchases to mitigate taxes.

Recommendations

  1. Cluster 0 - Moderately Active Spenders:
    • Proposed promotions could focus on periodic discounts or offers that encourage more frequent purchases, aligning with the relatively short time intervals between their previous purchases.
    • Recommending products based on their past purchases might be an effective strategy to further pique their interest.
  2. Cluster 1 - Infrequent Buyers with Small Purchases:
    • Promotions inviting them to acquire special discounts or purchase reminders could stimulate their interest.
    • Identifying their interests from previous purchases could aid in offering relevant and appealing products to this group.
  3. Cluster 2 - High-Spending Active Buyers:
    • They are quite active, but the purchasing intervals tend to be longer. Providing incentives or exclusive offers might be a good strategy.
    • Offering premium or exclusive products could be attractive to those inclined to make higher-value purchases.
  4. Cluster 3 - Highly Active, High-Spending Buyers:
    • This is a highly valuable customer segment. Strategies to maintain their loyalty need reinforcement through exclusive offers, frequent small discounts, or other incentives.
    • Ensuring a superior buying experience is also crucial to maintaining their satisfaction.
  5. Cluster 4 - Extreme Buyers:
    • They shop in significant volumes. Incentives for repeat purchases or exclusive product promotions might be highly appealing to them.
    • Considering specialized loyalty programs or rewards for frequent shoppers could be an effective strategy.
  6. Cluster 5 - Moderately Active with Moderate Purchases:
    • Providing promotions that encourage more frequent purchases aligned with the relatively short time intervals between their previous purchases.
    • Recommending products based on their interests derived from their past purchasing patterns could be a sound strategy.
