Customer Churn Prediction

Project Information

In this project, I created an analytical model to predict customers who are potentially inclined to switch from banking services. This involved analyzing transaction patterns and related factors to identify customers at high risk of churning.

Project Objectives

The main objective is to create a prediction model capable of forecasting potentially churned customers, aiming to reduce the losses caused by customer churn.

My Role

I was responsible as a data scientist for:

  1. Cleaning data from missing values and duplicate data.
  2. Performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to uncover insights and patterns.
  3. Performing data preprocessing to handle outliers.
  4. Engaging in feature engineering by creating new features, encoding features, and scaling.
  5. Building a supervised learning model for classification cases.
  6. Tuning and evaluating the model's performance.

Conclusion

Outcome

This model successfully predicted customers with potential churn with an accuracy score of 82%.

Conclusion

The developed model can assist stakeholders in devising retention strategies to reduce losses due to customer churn by predicting customers who are potentially inclined to churn.

Insights

  1. High Churn Impact Countries: Customers from France and Germany exhibit significantly higher churn rates.
  2. Gender's Role in Churn: Female customers tend to churn more frequently than male customers.
  3. Credit Card Usage and Churn: Credit card usage is associated with an increased churn rate.
  4. Inactive Customers Escalate Churn: Inactive customers show a high tendency to churn.
  5. Churn Trends and Product Usage: If customers use only one product, the churn rate tends to be higher; the fewer products used, the higher the churn rate.
  6. Relationship Length and Churn: Customers with a relationship length of 1-9 years are more likely to churn compared to longer and newer customers.
  7. $0 Balance and Balance Distribution: Many churned customers have a $0 balance, which constitutes the peak of the distribution. Additionally, there's a normal distribution in the balance range of $50,000 to $200,000, with an average of $91,108.54.
  8. Skews in Credit Score and Age: The distribution of Credit Score and Age for churned customers tends to be asymmetrical, with average and median Credit Scores at 645.35 and 646.00, respectively, while the average and median Ages are 44.84 (45 years) and 45 years.

Recommendations

  1. Market Segmentation (Customers from France and Germany):
    • Establish a loyalty program or special promotions considering the cultural preferences and shopping habits of customers from France and Germany.
    • Offer additional services or products focusing on the specific needs of these market segments. For instance, content or services translated into local languages or promotions tailored to local shopping preferences.
  2. Gender-based Personalization:
    • Tailor marketing communications and product offerings based on gender data. For example, more engaging promotions for each gender or exclusive offers relevant to each gender's preferences.
  3. Credit Card Policies and Services:
    • Enhance credit card policies by offering additional benefits aligned with customer needs and preferences. This might involve adjusting rewards or benefits offered, such as specific cashback for certain shopping categories.
  4. Reactivating Inactive Customers:
    • Prepare marketing programs targeting inactive customers. Offer special incentives, discounts, or service upgrades that may encourage them to return to using the services.
  5. Product or Service Bundles:
    • Create appealing product or service bundles for customers using only one product. For example, offer package discounts if they use more than one banking service or product.
  6. Special Care for 1-9 Year Relationship Customers:
    • Provide special attention to customers with a relationship duration of 1-9 years. For instance, offer loyalty programs, exclusive incentives, or additional services that make them feel appreciated.
  7. Financial Approach for $0 Balance Customers:
    • Provide financial solutions or additional services for customers with a $0 balance. This could involve investment product offers or financial advice to help them utilize the bank's services.
  8. Adjustments Based on Credit Score and Age Groups:
    • Develop marketing or retention strategies more aligned with customers' Credit Score profiles and ages. For instance, offer more relevant services or specific incentives appealing to certain age groups or credit score brackets.
